Subject-Verb Agreement - 32 Essential Rules
Home Letters S-V Agreement

1. Singular subjects take singular verbs

Example: She writes a letter every day.

2. Plural subjects take plural verbs

Example: They play football in the park.

3. Two singular subjects connected by 'and' take a plural verb

Example: Ravi and Sita are friends.

4. When two singular nouns are connected by 'or' or 'nor', use singular verb

Example: Neither Ram nor Shyam is coming today.

5. When a singular and plural subject are connected by 'or/nor', the verb agrees with the nearer subject

Example: Either the teacher or the students have submitted the list.

6. Indefinite pronouns like each, everyone, someone take singular verbs

Example: Each of the boys has a book.

7. Collective nouns usually take singular verb

Example: The team is winning the match.

8. Titles of books, movies, or organizations take singular verb

Example: "The Avengers" is a popular movie series.

9. The number vs. A number

Example: The number of students is increasing. / A number of students are present.

10. Words like news, mathematics, physics take singular verbs

Example: Mathematics is my favorite subject.

11. Plural form subjects with singular meaning take singular verbs

Example: Measles is a contagious disease.

12. Time, money, distance as a whole take singular verb

Example: Five hundred rupees is a big amount.

13. There is / There are - verb agrees with the subject after it

Example: There is a book on the table. / There are many books.

14. Some nouns are plural in form but singular in meaning

Example: Politics is not easy to understand.

15. Expressions like "as well as", "together with" don't affect verb

Example: The teacher, as well as students, is ready.

16. Use singular verb after 'each of', 'one of', etc.

Example: Each of the apples is rotten.

17. None can be singular or plural depending on meaning

Example: None of the cake is left. / None of the students are absent.

18. Subjects separated by commas or parentheses do not affect the verb

Example: My friend (along with her sisters) is here.

19. Fractions/percentages agree with the object of 'of'

Example: Half of the cake is gone. / Half of the students are absent.

20. In "either of/neither of" use singular verb

Example: Neither of the answers is correct.

21. Units of measurement usually take singular verbs

Example: Ten litres of water is needed.

22. Gerunds used as subjects take singular verbs

Example: Swimming is a good exercise.

23. Plural proper nouns (countries, institutions) take singular verb

Example: The United States is powerful.

24. When two subjects refer to the same person/thing, use singular

Example: My friend and guide is here.

25. The phrase 'the only one of' is followed by singular verb

Example: She is the only one of the girls who has qualified.

26. Names of sports teams take plural verbs

Example: India are playing well this season.

27. Plural nouns in book/movie titles take singular verb

Example: "The Chronicles of Narnia" is a famous book.

28. 'More than one' takes a singular verb

Example: More than one student is absent.

29. When the subject is "what" or "who", the verb depends on the answer

Example: What is needed is a new approach.

30. "One of those who" takes plural verb

Example: He is one of those who work hard.

31. Quantities like "ten kilometers", "five years" take singular verb

Example: Ten kilometers is a long distance.

32. "All" can be singular or plural depending on the object

Example: All the water is gone. / All the boys are present.
