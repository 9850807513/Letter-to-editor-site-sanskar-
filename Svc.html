Subject-Verb Agreement Rules

Rule 1:

A singular subject takes a singular verb. A plural subject takes a plural verb.
Example: She writes every day. / They write every day.

Rule 2:

Two singular subjects connected by "and" take a plural verb.
Example: Ram and Shyam are good friends.

Rule 3:

When two subjects are joined by "either/or" or "neither/nor", the verb agrees with the subject nearer to it.
Example: Neither the teacher nor the students were present.

Rule 4:

When words like "each", "every", "everyone", "someone", "nobody", etc. are used, they take a singular verb.
Example: Each of the boys was rewarded.

Rule 5:

Collective nouns like "team", "jury", "family" usually take a singular verb.
Example: The team is winning.

Rule 6:

With "a number of" use plural verb; with "the number of" use singular verb.
Example: A number of students are absent. / The number of students is increasing.

Rule 7:

When quantities and amounts are considered as a unit, use singular.
Example: Five hundred rupees is enough.

Rule 8:

Titles of books, movies, and novels take a singular verb.
Example: "The Wings of Fire" is an inspiring book.

Rule 9:

When the subject is a relative pronoun, the verb agrees with its antecedent.
Example: She is one of the girls who play basketball.

Rule 10:

With "there is/there are", the verb agrees with the noun that follows it.
Example: There is a book on the table. / There are books on the table.
