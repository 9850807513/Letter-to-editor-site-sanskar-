What are Letters?

Letters are written messages used to communicate information, feelings, or ideas. They can be handwritten or typed and are usually sent by post, email, or delivered in person. Letter writing is an essential skill, especially for official and formal communication.

Types of Letters

1. Formal Letters: Used for official communication like complaints, applications, and enquiries. These are structured and polite.

2. Informal Letters: Written to friends or family. These are casual and friendly.

3. Semi-formal Letters: Written to someone known but in a formal setup, like a teacher or neighbor.

Why Informal Letters are less used: With the rise of digital apps and messaging platforms, informal letters are no longer commonly written on paper. Most people use texts or social media for casual chats.

How to Write a Good Subject

The subject line gives a quick idea of the purpose of your letter. Keep it short, clear, and related to the content. Use proper keywords.

All Formal Letters

Complaint Letter

Purpose: To raise a concern regarding poor service or faulty products.

To,
The Municipal Commissioner,
ABC Nagar, Mumbai.

Subject: Complaint regarding unclean surroundings in ABC Nagar

Sir/Madam,

I am writing this letter to bring to your attention the extremely unhygienic conditions prevailing in our locality. The drains are overflowing, garbage remains uncollected, and streetlights do not function properly.

This has led to foul smells, mosquito breeding, and a rise in diseases. Children and senior citizens are especially at risk.

I request you to take urgent action by arranging for regular garbage pickup, proper drainage, and repairs. Your support will help in maintaining a healthy environment.

Yours sincerely,
Sanskar Shep

Leave Application

To,

Sanskar Shep

Letter to the Editor

To,
The Editor,
The Times of India,
Mumbai.

Subject: Increasing noise pollution in the city

Sir/Madam,

I wish to highlight the rising noise pollution due to loudspeakers and constant honking in our area. It's affecting health and concentration levels of students and elderly alike.

Despite clear rules, there seems to be no action. I request authorities to take strict steps to control this menace.

I hope this letter reaches the concerned departments and brings some relief.

Yours sincerely,
Sanskar Shep
