All Types of Letters

Letter to the Editor

What is it? A formal letter written to express opinions or raise issues in a newspaper.

Common Lines: Through the column of your esteemed newspaper...

Common Topics: Pollution, Traffic, Stray animals, Education system, etc.

CBSE PYQ: Write a letter to the editor of The Times of India about rising air pollution in your city.

Important Tips: Use formal tone, stay brief and focused, follow format strictly.

Example:

57, MG Road,
Pune - 411001
22nd July 2025

The Editor,
The Times of India,
Mumbai.

Subject: Increasing Air Pollution in Pune

Sir,
Through the column of your esteemed newspaper, I wish to draw attention to the rising level of air pollution in Pune. The situation is worsening each day due to the increase in vehicles, industrial smoke, and burning of waste. Citizens are suffering from respiratory problems, and children and the elderly are most affected. There is an urgent need for stricter traffic regulations, plantation drives, and awareness campaigns.

I hope my concern will find space in your paper and encourage authorities to take action.

Yours sincerely,
Sanskar Shep

Subject-Verb Agreement Rules (32 Total)

Rule 1: Singular subject takes singular verb.
Example: He goes to school.

Rule 2: Plural subject takes plural verb.
Example: They go to school.

Rule 3: When two singular subjects are connected by "and", the verb is plural.
Example: Ram and Shyam are playing.

Tense Rules (12 Tenses)

Present Simple: He plays daily. | Structure: Subject + base verb(s/es)

Past Simple: He played yesterday. | Structure: Subject + V2

Future Simple: He will play tomorrow. | Structure: Subject + will + base verb
