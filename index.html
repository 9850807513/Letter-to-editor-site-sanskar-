Class 7 Science – Acids, Bases and Salts

Introduction

Acids: Sour in taste, turn blue litmus → red. Examples: HCl, H₂SO₄, lemon juice.

Bases: Bitter, soapy, turn red litmus → blue. Examples: NaOH, lime water, baking soda.

Salts: Formed by reaction of acid + base. Example: NaCl, CaCO₃.

Indicators

  • Litmus: Acid → red, Base → blue
  • Methyl Orange: Acid → red, Base → yellow
  • Phenolphthalein: Acid → colourless, Base → pink
  • Turmeric: Acid → yellow, Base → reddish-brown
  • China rose: Acid → dark pink, Base → green

Important Points

  • Strong acids: HCl, H₂SO₄. Weak acids: Acetic acid.
  • Strong bases: NaOH, KOH. Weak bases: NH₄OH.
  • Neutralisation: Acid + Base → Salt + Water (+ Heat).
  • Used in digestion, agriculture (neutralising soil), medicine (antacids).

Class 8 History – Trade to Territory

European Arrival

1498 – Vasco da Gama reached Calicut, opening sea route to India. Portuguese, Dutch, French, and British traders arrived seeking trade and influence.

British Expansion

1600 – East India Company established trading centres in Surat, Madras, Bombay, and Calcutta. They signed treaties with local rulers, gained trading rights, and started collecting revenue.

Important Battles & Dates

  • Battle of Plassey (1757): British defeated Siraj-ud-Daulah, gained Bengal control.
  • Battle of Buxar (1764): British defeated Shuja-ud-Daula, Mir Qasim, Shah Alam II; Bengal & Bihar consolidated.
  • Anglo-Mysore Wars (1767–1799): Tipu Sultan defeated & died in 4th war (1799); Mysore annexed.
  • Anglo-Maratha Wars (1775–1818): Maratha power ended; British expanded territory.
  • Anglo-Sikh Wars (1845–49): Punjab annexed to British India.

Administrative Changes

  • 1765 – Diwani rights in Bengal, Bihar, Orissa; revenue collection by Company.
  • Company maintained army, introduced British laws and administration.
  • Shifted from trade to territorial control → early British Empire in India.
  • Impact: Economy, land revenue systems, social structure transformed.

Class 9 Science – Gravitation

Gravitation

Every object in the universe attracts every other object with a force called Gravitational Force. Universal force acts between all objects.

Newton’s Law of Gravitation

Force between two objects is directly proportional to product of their masses and inversely proportional to square of distance.

Formula: F = G × (m₁ × m₂) / r², G = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ Nm²/kg².

Free Fall

Objects fall only under gravity. Acceleration g = 9.8 m/s² on Earth.

Mass & Weight

Mass: Constant quantity of matter. Weight: W = m × g, changes with gravity.

Thrust & Pressure

Pressure = Force / Area. Broad area → less pressure. Example: Camel feet.

Buoyancy & Archimedes’ Principle

Buoyant force = weight of displaced fluid. Applications: Ships, hydrometers, submarines.

Relative Density

Density of substance ÷ Density of water. No units.

Einstein’s Contribution

Mass bends space-time; planets move along curves. Example: Moon revolves around Earth.

Important Points

  • Gravity controls universe structure.
  • Planets revolve around Sun due to gravity.
  • Satellites stay in orbit due to speed & gravity balance.
  • Artificial satellites: communication, GPS, weather, defence.
  • Without gravity, life, atmosphere, oceans wouldn’t exist.
