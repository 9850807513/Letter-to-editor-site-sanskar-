Subject-Verb Agreement (21 Rules with Extended Explanations)

1. Singular subjects take singular verbs:
Example: He plays football daily.
2. Plural subjects take plural verbs:
Example: They play football daily.
3. Two subjects joined by "and" take a plural verb:
Example: Ram and Shyam are best friends.
4. If two subjects refer to the same person/thing, use a singular verb:
Example: The poet and philosopher is dead.
5. Either/Neither is singular:
Example: Neither of the boys has completed the homework.
6. Each/Everyone/Anybody is singular:
Example: Everyone is happy.
7. Words like many, few, both are plural:
Example: Both are coming to the party.
8. In sentences starting with "There/Here", the verb agrees with the real subject:
Example: There is a book on the table.
9. Collective nouns usually take singular verbs:
Example: The team is winning.
10. A plural noun with a singular meaning takes a singular verb:
Example: Mathematics is difficult.
11. When distances, periods, sums of money are considered as a unit:
Example: Ten rupees is not enough.
12. Some nouns ending in -s are singular:
Example: News is spreading fast.
13. With "a number of", use a plural verb:
Example: A number of students have failed.
14. With "the number of", use a singular verb:
Example: The number of students is increasing.
15. Fractions and percentages agree with the noun after "of":
Example: One-third of the cake is gone.
16. Plural forms of countries or books are singular:
Example: The United States is powerful.
17. Titles of books/movies take singular verb:
Example: "Gulliver’s Travels" is an interesting book.
18. "One of" followed by plural noun takes a singular verb:
Example: One of the girls is missing.
19. Expressions like "as well as", "along with" do not make the subject plural:
Example: The teacher, along with students, is present.
20. Nouns plural in form but singular in meaning:
Example: Politics is interesting.
21. With "none", both singular and plural are acceptable based on context:
Example: None of the cookies are left. / None of the work is done.

Tense Rules

Present Tense

  • Simple Present: I walk to school.
  • Present Continuous: I am walking to school.
  • Present Perfect: I have walked to school.
  • Present Perfect Continuous: I have been walking to school.

Past Tense

  • Simple Past: I walked to school.
  • Past Continuous: I was walking to school.
  • Past Perfect: I had walked to school.
  • Past Perfect Continuous: I had been walking to school.

Future Tense

  • Simple Future: I will walk to school.
  • Future Continuous: I will be walking to school.
  • Future Perfect: I will have walked to school.
  • Future Perfect Continuous: I will have been walking to school.
