Q1. I speak without a mouth and hear without ears. I have nobody, but I come alive with wind.

  • A) Whisper
  • B) Echo
  • C) Shadow
  • D) Reflection
Answer: B) Echo
Echo is a reflected sound; it “speaks” and “hears” without physical organs.

Q2. Which planet rotates in the opposite direction compared to most planets in the solar system?

  • A) Earth
  • B) Venus
  • C) Mars
  • D) Jupiter
Answer: B) Venus
Venus rotates clockwise (retrograde) unlike most planets.

Q3. Find the value of √(50 + 20√6) - √(50 - 20√6).

  • A) 2
  • B) 4
  • C) 4√5
  • D) 6
Answer: C) 4√5
(√(50+20√6) − √(50−20√6))² = 100 − 2√100 = 80 → √80 = 4√5

Q4. Famous book of Rabindranath Tagore that won Nobel Prize?

  • A) Chokher Bali

Q5. Two identical thermometers: one in vacuum, one in air. Which responds faster?

  • A) Vacuum
  • B) Air
  • C) Both same
  • D) Cannot say
Answer: B) Air
Air allows conduction and convection, transferring heat faster than vacuum.

Q6. Right-angled triangle has legs 6 cm and 8 cm. Square on hypotenuse. Area?

  • A) 80 cm²
  • B) 90 cm²
  • C) 100 cm²
  • D) 120 cm²
Answer: C) 100 cm²
Hypotenuse = √(6² + 8²) = 10 → Area of square = 10² = 100.

Q8. A metal wire is heated and expands. If a weight is hanging from it, what happens to tension?

  • A) Increases
  • B) Decreases
  • C) Remains same
  • D) Becomes zero
Answer: B) Decreases
Thermal expansion reduces stretching caused by the weight, lowering tension slightly.

Q9. A square side 10 cm. Cut 4 corners of 2 cm each. Remaining area?

  • A) 80 cm²
  • B) 84 cm²
  • C) 88 cm²
  • D) 90 cm²
Answer: B) 84 cm²
Original area = 100, removed 16 → Remaining = 84.

Q10. If x + 1/x = 5, what is x² + 1/x²?

  • A) 21
  • B) 23
  • C) 25
  • D) 27
Answer: B) 23
(x + 1/x)² = x² + 1/x² + 2 → 5² = x² + 1/x² + 2 → x² + 1/x² = 23.

Q11. Which country launched the first artificial satellite?

  • A) USA
  • B) USSR
  • C) China
  • D) Japan
Answer: B) USSR
Sputnik 1, launched by USSR in 1957, was the first artificial satellite.

Q12. Two metal blocks of same material, one hot and one cold, placed in contact. Heat flows from?

  • A) Cold to Hot
  • B) Hot to Cold
  • C) No heat flow
  • D) Depends on mass
Answer: B) Hot to Cold
Heat always flows from higher temperature to lower temperature until equilibrium.

Q13. Backward spin in cricket causes ball to:

  • A) Go straight
  • B) Deviate opposite way to normal spin
  • C) Bounce less
  • D) Stop suddenly
Answer: B) Deviate opposite way to normal spin
Backward spin or backspin causes the ball to reverse its usual curve after bouncing.

Q14. 3L and 5L vessels, only these available. How to measure exactly 4L?

  • A) Fill 5L, pour into 3L, repeat
  • B) Impossible
  • C) Fill 3L, pour into 5L twice
  • D) None
Answer: A) Fill 5L, pour into 3L, repeat
Classic jug problem: fill 5L, pour into 3L (leaving 2L in 5L), empty 3L, pour 2L into 3L, fill 5L again, pour 1L into 3L → 4L remains in 5L.

Q15. Which is the only food that never spoils?

  • A) Sugar
  • B) Honey
  • C) Salt
  • D) Rice
Answer: B) Honey
Honey has natural preservatives and low water content; it can last indefinitely if sealed properly.

Q16. Riddle: I’m tall when I’m young, and short when I’m old. What am I?

  • A) Candle
  • B) Tree
  • C) Pencil
  • D) Mountain
Answer: A) Candle
A candle shortens as it burns, tall when new and young.

Q17. Solve quickly: 25 + √225 + 36 / 6

  • A) 40
  • B) 46
  • C) 50
  • D) 55
Answer: B) 46
√225 = 15, 36/6 = 6 → 25 + 15 + 6 = 46.
