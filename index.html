Letter to Editor - Complete Guide

1. What is a Letter?

A letter is a written form of communication where one person writes a message to another. It can be formal or informal based on the purpose and recipient.

2. Types of Letters

  • Formal Letter: Used for official purposes (e.g., to the editor, principal, company).
  • Informal Letter: Personal letter written to friends or family.

3. Most Asked Letter (from 2007 till now)

Letter to the Editor is one of the most frequently asked types of letters in board exams since 2007.

4. How to Write a Proper Subject for Letter to Editor

  • Keep it short and specific.
  • Example: \"Rising Pollution Levels in Cities\"
  • Focus on the main issue you are raising.

5. Format of Letter to the Editor

Sender's Address
Date
The Editor
Newspaper Name
City

Subject: [Topic of the letter]

Sir/Madam,

[Body - Introduction, Problem, Suggestions, Conclusion]

Thank you
Yours sincerely,
[Name]

6. Common Points Used to Write Letter to Editor

  • State the issue clearly.
  • Mention its impact on public/society.
  • Give logical suggestions or solutions.
  • Use formal and polite language.

7. Example Letter Using Above Points

24 Green Park Colony
Ambajogai
25 July 2025

The Editor
The Times of India
Mumbai

Subject: Need for Stricter Waste Management in Urban Areas

Sir/Madam,

Through the columns of your esteemed newspaper, I want to highlight the serious issue of poor waste management in urban areas. The overflowing garbage and lack of proper disposal systems are causing health hazards.

This issue affects not just the environment but also the daily lives of citizens. It is high time authorities take strict measures, such as better garbage collection, awareness campaigns, and fines for littering.

I hope you will publish this letter to draw attention to this concern.

Yours sincerely,
Sanskar Patil
