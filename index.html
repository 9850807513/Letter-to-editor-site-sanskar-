From Trade to Territory – Class 8 History

📘 Detailed Notes

1. Arrival of Europeans

The Portuguese were the first Europeans to arrive in India when Vasco da Gama landed in Calicut in 1498. Soon, Dutch, French and English traders followed. The East India Company was established in England in 1600. By 1651, the English had set up their first factory in Hooghly (Bengal). Gradually, they established trading posts in Surat, Madras, Bombay, and Calcutta.

2. Carnatic Wars (1746–1763)

The English and French fought three Carnatic Wars for control of trade and territory in South India.

  • 1st War (1746–1748): No clear winner.
  • 2nd War (1749–1754): French defeated, British gained influence.
  • 3rd War (1756–1763): British won, French lost all political power in India.
3. Bengal and the Battles of Plassey & Buxar

Battle of Plassey (1757): Siraj-ud-Daulah, Nawab of Bengal, was defeated by the British under Robert Clive due to betrayal by Mir Jafar. Bengal came under Company control.

Battle of Buxar (1764): The Company defeated the alliance of Mir Qasim (Bengal), Shuja-ud-Daulah (Oudh), and Mughal Emperor Shah Alam II. This made the Company supreme in Bengal.

Treaty of Allahabad (1765): The Company gained Diwani rights (tax collection) of Bengal, Bihar, and Orissa.

4. Mysore Wars (1767–1799)

Mysore under Hyder Ali and Tipu Sultan became powerful. Four Anglo-Mysore Wars were fought:

  • 1st (1767–1769): Ended without result.
  • 2nd (1780–1784): Treaty of Mangalore signed.
  • 3rd (1790–1792): Tipu lost half his territory.
  • 4th (1799): Tipu killed → Mysore fell to British.
5. Maratha Wars (1775–1818)
  • 1st (1775–1782): Treaty of Salbai → no change.
  • 2nd (1803–1805): British gained large territories.
  • 3rd (1817–1818): Marathas finally defeated → British supremacy in India.
6. Policies of Annexation

Subsidiary Alliance (1798): Introduced by Lord Wellesley. Indian rulers forced to accept British troops and pay for their maintenance. Hyderabad was first to accept it.

Doctrine of Lapse (1848): Introduced by Lord Dalhousie. If a ruler died without a male heir, his kingdom was annexed. Satara, Jhansi, and Nagpur were annexed this way.

7. End of Company Rule

By 1856, the British controlled almost all of India. The oppressive policies and expansionist measures caused great resentment. This finally led to the Revolt of 1857, the first major rebellion against British rule.

📘 Gravitation – Class 9 Notes

NCERT Concepts + Einstein’s Gravity (Extra)

🔹 Universal Law of Gravitation

Every object in the universe attracts every other object with a force which is directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

Formula: F = G × (m₁ × m₂) / r²

Universal Gravitational Constant (G) = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ Nm²/kg²

🔹 Free Fall

Whenever an object falls towards Earth under the influence of gravitational force alone, it is said to be in free fall.

Acceleration due to gravity (g) on Earth = 9.8 m/s².

🔹 Mass and Weight

  • Mass: Quantity of matter in a body (constant everywhere).
  • Weight: Force with which Earth attracts a body. (W = m × g)
  • Weight on Moon = 1/6 of Earth’s weight.

🔹 Potential & Kinetic Energy

Potential Energy (PE) = m × g × h

Kinetic Energy (KE) = ½ m v²

Law of Conservation of Energy: Energy can neither be created nor destroyed, only changes form.

🔹 Pressure in Fluids & Archimedes’ Principle

  • Fluids exert pressure in all directions.
  • Pressure increases with depth.
  • Archimedes’ Principle: Buoyant force = Weight of displaced fluid.

Applications: Ships, submarines, hydrometers, hot-air balloons.

📜 Timeline of Discoveries

1687: Newton discovers Universal Law of Gravitation.

1915: Einstein proposes General Theory of Relativity.

1969: Astronauts on Moon experience 1/6th gravity.

Present: Gravity research continues in black holes & space science.

🌌 Einstein’s Gravity (Extra Knowledge)

  • Newton: Gravity = Force of attraction.
  • Einstein: Gravity = Bending of space-time.
  • Massive objects like Earth and Sun bend space-time → smaller objects move around them.
  • Explains: bending of light near stars, planets revolving around Sun.

📝 Quick Summary

  • F = G m₁m₂ / r²
  • g = 9.8 m/s² (varies with height & depth)
  • Mass constant, Weight = m g (changes with place)
  • PE = m g h, KE = ½ m v²
  • Archimedes’ Principle → Buoyant force = displaced fluid
  • Einstein: Gravity = Bending of space-time
