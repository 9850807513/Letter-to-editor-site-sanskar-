Acids, Bases and Salts

1. Acids

Definition: Substances that have a sour taste and turn blue litmus red.

  • Examples: Hydrochloric acid (HCl), Sulphuric acid (H₂SO₄), Citric acid (lemon).
  • Indicators: Litmus, Methyl orange, Phenolphthalein.

2. Bases

Definition: Substances that taste bitter, feel soapy, turn red litmus blue.

  • Examples: Sodium hydroxide (NaOH), Lime water (Ca(OH)₂), Baking soda (NaHCO₃).
  • Indicators: Phenolphthalein → pink in base, Methyl orange → yellow in base.

3. Salts

Definition: Products of neutralization of acid and base.

Example: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O

4. Indicators & Colour Change

  • Litmus: Acid → Red, Base → Blue
  • Methyl Orange: Acid → Red, Base → Yellow
  • Phenolphthalein: Acid → Colourless, Base → Pink
  • Turmeric: Acid → Yellow, Base → Reddish brown

Trade to Territory – The Company Establishes Power

1. European Traders in India

  • 1498 – Vasco da Gama reached Calicut (Portuguese).
  • 1600 – British East India Company established.
  • 1602 – Dutch East India Company formed.
  • 1664 – French East India Company formed.

2. Early Company Power

Company set up trading centres: Surat, Madras, Bombay, Calcutta. Signed treaties with local rulers → trading rights.

3. Major Battles & Dates

  • Battle of Plassey (1757): Robert Clive defeated Nawab Siraj-ud-Daulah → Bengal under Company.
  • Battle of Buxar (1764): British defeated Shuja-ud-Daulah, Mir Qasim, Shah Alam II → Diwani rights in Bengal, Bihar, Orissa.
  • Anglo-Mysore Wars (1767–1799): Tipu Sultan defeated & died in 4th war → Mysore annexed.
  • Anglo-Maratha Wars (1775–1818): Maratha power ended → Company expanded territory.
  • Anglo-Sikh Wars (1845–1849): Punjab annexed.

4. Administrative Changes

  • Company collected revenue, maintained army, introduced British laws.
  • Governor-General introduced → centralised control.
  • Shift from trade to territorial control → early British Empire in India.
  • Economic, social, and political systems changed.

Gravitation – Detailed NCERT/Schand Notes

1. Gravitation

Gravitation is the universal force of attraction between any two objects in the universe.

2. Gravity

Gravity is the force by which Earth attracts objects toward its center. Special case of gravitation.

3. Newton’s Law of Gravitation

“Every object in the universe attracts every other object with a force proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of distance.”

Formula: F = G × (m₁ × m₂) / r²

G = 6.67 × 10⁻¹¹ Nm²/kg²

4. Free Fall

Objects fall under gravity alone → acceleration g = 9.8 m/s².

5. Mass & Weight

  • Mass: Quantity of matter, constant. SI → kg
  • Weight: W = m × g, changes with gravity.

6. Thrust & Pressure

Pressure = Force/Area. Smaller area → higher pressure.

7. Buoyancy & Archimedes’ Principle

Upthrust on body = weight of displaced fluid.

Applications: ships, hydrometers, submarines, life jackets.

8. Relative Density

Relative Density = Density of substance ÷ Density of water. RD >1 → sinks, RD <1 → floats.

9. Motion of Planets

Planets revolve around Sun due to gravitation; Moon revolves around Earth.

10. Satellites

Natural: Moon, Artificial: communication, GPS, defence, research.

11. Einstein’s Contribution

Mass bends space-time → gravitation explained as curvature of space-time.

12. Importance of Gravitation

  • Keeps planets, satellites, atmosphere in orbit.
  • Responsible for tides, rainfall, oceans.
  • Without gravity → life impossible.
