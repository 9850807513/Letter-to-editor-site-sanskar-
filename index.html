Class 7 – Acids, Bases & Salts

Acid: Sour in taste, turns blue litmus red.

Base: Bitter, soapy, turns red litmus blue.

Salt: Formed by neutralisation of acid and base.

Indicators

  • Litmus: Acid → Red, Base → Blue
  • Turmeric: Acid → Yellow, Base → Reddish brown
  • China Rose: Acid → Dark pink/red, Base → Green
  • Phenolphthalein: Acid → Colorless, Base → Pink
  • Methyl Orange: Acid → Red, Base → Yellow
  • Universal Indicator: pH scale (0-14), Neutral = 7 (green)
Summary: Acids and bases identified using indicators. Salts formed by neutralisation. pH scale defines acidity/basicity.

Class 8 – From Trade to Territory

1600 – East India Company formed.

1615 – Sir Thomas Roe got Farman from Jahangir.

1717 – Farrukhsiyar granted duty-free trade in Bengal.

Battles

  • 1757 – Battle of Plassey (Company vs Siraj-ud-Daulah → Company won Bengal)
  • 1764 – Battle of Buxar (Company defeated Mir Qasim, Shuja-ud-Daulah, Shah Alam II)
  • 1767–69 – First Anglo-Mysore War
  • 1780–84 – Second Anglo-Mysore War
  • 1790–92 – Third Anglo-Mysore War
  • 1799 – Fourth Anglo-Mysore War (Tipu Sultan killed)
  • 1775–82 – First Anglo-Maratha War
  • 1803–05 – Second Anglo-Maratha War
  • 1817–19 – Third Anglo-Maratha War (Marathas defeated)

Expansion Policies

1798 – Subsidiary Alliance (Lord Wellesley)

1848 – Doctrine of Lapse (Lord Dalhousie)

1856 – Awadh annexed

Summary: From traders to rulers, British won battles (Plassey 1757, Buxar 1764, Mysore, Marathas). Used Subsidiary Alliance & Doctrine of Lapse for expansion.

Class 9 – Gravitation

Gravitation: Force of attraction between any two masses in universe.

Newton’s Law: F = G m₁m₂ / r²

Free fall: Motion under gravity alone (acceleration = g).

Mass & Weight: Weight = m × g; varies with gravity, mass remains same.

Thrust & Pressure: Pressure = Force/Area.

Archimedes’ Principle: Buoyant force = weight of displaced liquid.

Relative Density: Density of substance ÷ Density of water.

Einstein’s Contribution

Gravity is bending of space-time caused by mass. Explains planetary motion more accurately than Newton’s law.

Summary: Gravitation explained by Newton’s law, free fall, Archimedes’ principle, and Einstein’s space-time concept.
