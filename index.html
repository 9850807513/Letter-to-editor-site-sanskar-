Formal Letter Types

Letter to the Editor

Definition: A formal letter written to the editor of a newspaper/magazine to express opinions or highlight issues.

Common Lines: "Through the columns of your esteemed newspaper..."

Common Topics: Pollution, Traffic, Public Awareness, etc.

PYQ Example: Write a letter to the editor about the rising use of plastic in your city.

Important Tips: Be precise, stick to facts, and use formal tone.

Example:

To,
The Editor,
The Times of India,
Delhi.
Date: 10th July 2025

Subject: Growing Plastic Pollution in Our City

Sir/Madam,

Through the columns of your esteemed newspaper, I wish to draw the attention of the authorities and the public towards the growing menace of plastic pollution in our locality. Despite various awareness campaigns, plastic bags and waste can be seen littered across streets and parks. This not only pollutes the environment but also endangers the lives of animals who often consume this plastic unknowingly.

Strict action must be taken to enforce the plastic ban. Fines should be imposed and awareness programs intensified. Schools and colleges should educate students on using eco-friendly alternatives. I hope this issue will be published in your newspaper to spread mass awareness.

Yours sincerely,
Sanskar Shep

Subject-Verb Agreement (SVA) Rules

Rule 1: Singular Subject → Singular Verb

Example: He plays cricket.

Rule 2: Plural Subject → Plural Verb

Example: They play cricket.

Tense Rules

1. Simple Present Tense

Structure: Subject + V1 (+ s/es)

Example: She sings a song.

2. Present Continuous Tense

Structure: Subject + is/am/are + V1+ing

Example: I am reading a book.
