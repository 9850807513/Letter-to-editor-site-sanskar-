Letter to the Editor

1. What is it?
A formal letter written to a newspaper or magazine editor to express opinions, raise issues, or suggest improvements.

2. Common Lines:
I would like to draw attention to..., Through the columns of your esteemed newspaper...

3. Common Topics:
Pollution, traffic issues, water crisis, examination pressure, safety in schools.

4. PYQ (CBSE):
Write a letter to the editor of a newspaper highlighting the problems caused by open garbage dumps in your locality.

5. Important Tips:
Be polite and concise, clearly mention the issue, and suggest possible solutions.

Example:

To,
The Editor,
The Times of India,
Delhi

Subject: Need to improve road safety for school children

Sir/Madam,
Through the columns of your esteemed newspaper, I would like to draw attention to the increasing number of road accidents involving school-going children in our city. The lack of traffic signals, zebra crossings near schools, and negligent driving by auto-rickshaw drivers are a major concern for parents and teachers. Children’s safety must be the highest priority for all concerned authorities. I request the municipal corporation and traffic department to take prompt actions such as installing speed breakers, strict patrolling, and road safety campaigns in school zones. Hope this issue will receive your prompt attention.

Yours sincerely,
Sanskar Shep

Complaint Letter

1. What is it?
A formal letter written to raise a complaint to authorities regarding any issue.

2. Common Lines:
I am writing this letter to express dissatisfaction..., I wish to lodge a complaint...

3. Common Topics:
Defective products, water supply issues, poor school facilities, delayed service.

4. PYQ (CBSE):
Write a letter to the Municipal Commissioner complaining about the overflowing drainage system.

5. Tips:
Mention problem clearly, include date/details, suggest a remedy.

Example:

To,
The Municipal Commissioner,
Ambajogai Municipal Council,
Beed, Maharashtra

Subject: Complaint about overflowing drainage and mosquito menace

Sir/Madam,
I would like to bring to your kind notice that the drainage system in Bhusnar locality is severely choked, leading to overflowing drains and unhygienic conditions. The stagnant water has become a breeding ground for mosquitoes, causing fear of diseases like dengue and malaria. The local residents have made several verbal complaints, but no action has been taken. I request you to initiate cleaning operations immediately and ensure regular maintenance. A prompt resolution is urgently required to safeguard public health.

Yours sincerely,
Sanskar Shep

Enquiry Letter

1. What is it?
Used to request information about services, courses, or products.

2. Common Lines:
I wish to enquire about..., Kindly provide me with details...

3. Common Topics:
Course enquiry, hostel facilities, tour packages.

4. PYQ (CBSE):
Write a letter to XYZ Coaching Institute enquiring about Class 10 Science course.

5. Tips:
Be clear and specific, request brochures or contact details if needed.

Example:

To,
The Director,
Brilliant Coaching Institute,
Pune

Subject: Enquiry about Class 10 Science coaching

Sir/Madam,
I am a student of Class 10 and wish to join your reputed institute for Science preparation. I would like to know the batch timings, fee structure, duration of the course, and study material provided. Kindly let me know if weekend batches are available as well. I will be grateful if you could send me the details via email or WhatsApp. Thanking you in advance.

Yours sincerely,
Sanskar Shep

Order Letter

1. What is it?
A letter written to place an order for goods, usually by institutions or schools.

2. Common Lines:
We would like to place an order for..., Kindly dispatch the following items...

3. Common Topics:
Books, sports goods, lab materials.

4. PYQ (CBSE):
Write a letter placing order for sports items for your school.

5. Tips:
List items clearly, mention quantity, mode of payment, and delivery date.

Example:

To,
The Sales Manager,
Sports World Pvt. Ltd.
Mumbai

Subject: Order for sports items

Sir/Madam,
We would like to place an order for the following sports items for our school:
- 10 footballs
- 12 badminton rackets
- 5 cricket kits
Please ensure good quality products and timely delivery before 10th August. The payment will be made via cheque after delivery.

Yours faithfully,
Sanskar Shep
Sports Secretary, Green Field School

Request/Application Letter

1. What is it?
Written to request permissions or services from officials or authorities.

2. Common Lines:
I request you to kindly..., I am writing this letter to request...

3. Common Topics:
Requesting bonafide certificate, permission to attend camp, etc.

4. PYQ (CBSE):
Write an application requesting to issue a character certificate.

5. Tips:
Keep it polite and to the point, mention purpose clearly.

Example:

To,
The Principal,
Saraswati Vidyalaya,
Ambajogai

Subject: Request for character certificate

Sir/Madam,
I am a student of Class 10 and need a character certificate for applying to a scholarship program. I kindly request you to issue the certificate at the earliest. I have enclosed my identity proof and mark sheet along with the application.

Thanking you,
Sanskar Shep

Leave Application

1. What is it?
Written to request leave for personal, medical, or urgent reasons.

2. Common Lines:
I request you to grant me leave from..., I am unable to attend school due to...

3. Common Topics:
Sick leave, family function, personal work.

4. PYQ (CBSE):
Write an application requesting three days' leave due to illness.

5. Tips:
Mention reason and duration clearly, attach documents if required.

Example:

To,
The Class Teacher,
Modern Public School,
Ambajogai

Subject: Leave application for three days

Sir/Madam,
I am suffering from viral fever and have been advised complete rest. Kindly grant me leave from 21st to 23rd July. I assure you that I will complete the missed work after joining back.

Thanking you,
Sanskar Shep

21 Rules of Subject-Verb Agreement

  1. Singular subjects take singular verbs. (He runs.)
  2. Plural subjects take plural verbs. (They play.)
  3. Subjects joined by 'and' take plural verbs. (Tom and Jerry are friends.)
  4. If subjects are joined by 'or/nor', the verb agrees with the nearest subject.
  5. Each, everyone, either, neither take singular verbs.
  6. Uncountable nouns take singular verbs. (Water is essential.)
  7. Collective nouns may take singular/plural depending on context.
  8. Distances, periods, and amounts take singular verbs.
  9. Titles of books, movies take singular verbs.
  10. The number vs A number: "The number" is singular, "A number" is plural.
  11. None can be singular/plural based on context.
  12. Subjects with fractions/percentages depend on noun after 'of'.
  13. There is/There are - verb agrees with subject after 'there'.
  14. Singular nouns with plural meaning take plural verbs. (Scissors are sharp.)
  15. Expressions like 'with', 'as well as' do not affect subject-verb.
  16. Gerunds take singular verbs. (Swimming is fun.)
  17. Indefinite pronouns like "someone", "anybody" take singular verbs.
  18. Plural form subjects with singular meaning (e.g., Mathematics) take singular verbs.
  19. Sentences beginning with 'what' or 'how' take verb based on subject.
  20. Some nouns look plural but are singular (e.g., News).
  21. Plural subjects acting as one unit take singular verbs. (Bread and butter is good.)

Tense Rules

  • Simple Present: He eats. (Habit, general truth)
  • Present Continuous: He is eating. (Now, temporary)
  • Present Perfect: He has eaten. (Completed just now)
  • Present Perfect Continuous: He has been eating. (Since/for time)
  • Simple Past: He ate. (Completed in past)
  • Past Continuous: He was eating. (Ongoing past)
  • Past Perfect: He had eaten. (Before another action)
  • Past Perfect Continuous: He had been eating. (Duration before something in past)
  • Simple Future: He will eat. (Later)
  • Future Continuous: He will be eating. (Ongoing in future)
  • Future Perfect: He will have eaten. (Completed in future)
  • Future Perfect Continuous: He will have been eating. (Duration up to a point in future)
